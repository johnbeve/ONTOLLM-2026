ONTOLLM 2026

3rd Annual Workshop on the Convergence of Large Language Models and Ontologies

Joint Ontology Workshops (JOWO XII) · Co-located with FOIS 2026
21–25 September 2026

Overview Topics Submissions Important Dates Organizers

Workshop Overview

The accelerating deployment of Large Language Models (LLMs) has renewed interest in ontologies and knowledge graphs as mechanisms for semantic grounding, explainability, interoperability, and trust. Ontologies provide explicit structure and formal constraints, while LLMs offer generative capacity and flexible language-based reasoning.

This workshop focuses on the convergence of symbolic and sub-symbolic approaches, addressing challenges such as aligning LLM outputs with ontological constraints, improving explainability, integrating hybrid AI architectures, and evaluating vulnerabilities in ontology–LLM systems.

Topics of Interest

  • Ontological principles in LLM design
  • LLM-assisted ontology generation, refinement, and alignment
  • Hybrid ontology–LLM architectures
  • Ontology-driven data integration for LLM workflows
  • Explainability and interpretability using ontologies
  • Ontology-based natural language understanding
  • Domain-specific ontology–LLM applications
  • Ethical and epistemic issues in symbolic–sub-symbolic AI
We welcome researchers from all career stages to participate, work in progress (short papers) are also welcome as the primary goal of the workshop is discussion. All papers must be original and not submitted to, or accepted by, any other workshop, conference or journal. Note that, for inclusion in the JOWO proceedings, short and position papers are required to be at least 5 pages long. All contributions will be peer-reviewed, and the review process will be managed in a collaborative and transparent manner using the EasyChair System as part of the JOWO organisation.

Submission Guidelines

Paper Types

  • Abstracts: 2-3 pages, 10-15 minute presentations, not included in the proceedings
  • Short papers: 5–6, pages, 10-15 minute presentations
  • Full papers: 10–14, pages, 20-25 minute presentations

Formatting

Submissions must use the CEUR-ART 1-column format and be submitted as a single PDF via EasyChair. Authors are strongly encouraged to use the latex template here. Papers should be non-anonymized, following JOWO requirements.
Please make submissions using the link: https://easychair.org/my/conference?conf=fois2026 (Select the track JOWO Workshop – ONTOLLM).

Required Declarations & Files

  • Declaration on the use of Generative AI (see policy here)
  • Camera-ready source files
  • Signed CEUR author agreement (please choose the correct "Author Agreement Variant")

For inclusion in the workshop, at least one of the authors of accepted papers needs to register at JOWO 2025 and participate on-site at ONTOLLM.
Properly formatted accepted papers will be published in the joint CEUR-WS JOWO 2026 proceedings.
